## Open Bar: The Gold Standard

The open bar is what most people think of when they picture a "real" corporate party. Company pays for everything, guests drink what they want, everyone's happy. But there's more to consider than just signing the check.

**Pros:**
- Shows genuine appreciation for your team
- No awkward payment moments
- Guests can focus on networking, not their wallets
- Creates a more festive, celebratory atmosphere

**Cons:**
- Budget can spiral if not managed carefully
- Some guests may overindulge
- Higher upfront commitment

**Real Example:** Last quarter, we helped a tech company in the Domain throw a 200-person product launch party at the Fairmont. They went with a 3-hour open bar featuring craft cocktails and local Austin beers. Total cost: about $8,500, which worked out to $42.50 per person. The client told us it was worth every penny for the team morale boost.

## Cash Bar: Use with Caution

![Content Image](/images/blog/corporate-event-bar-service-options/corporate-event-bar-service-1.webp)

Let's be honest—cash bars can feel a bit cheap for corporate events. That said, there are situations where they make perfect sense.

**When Cash Bars Work:**
- All-staff events with 200+ employees
- Industry conferences or networking mixers
- Events where alcohol isn't the focus
- Organizations with strict budget constraints

**Pro Tip from Experience:** If you're going cash bar, at least provide the first round on the company. We've seen this small gesture completely change the vibe of an event. Costs about $5-8 per person for a welcome drink, but the goodwill is priceless.

## Drink Tickets: The Goldilocks Option

Drink tickets strike that perfect balance between generosity and budget control. Each guest gets 2-3 tickets good for beer, wine, or cocktails. Want more? Cash bar from there.

**Why We Love This Option:**
- Predictable costs (multiply tickets × guests × drink price)
- Shows you're providing, while setting reasonable limits
- Reduces overconsumption concerns
- Works great for 3-4 hour events

**Budget Breakdown Example:**
- 100 employees
- 3 tickets each = 300 total
- $12 average per drink
- Total cost: $3,600

Compare that to an open bar at $35/person ($3,500) but with way more control over consumption and costs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What's the average cost for corporate event bar service in Austin?

A: For open bar packages, expect $25-45 per person for 2-3 hours. This typically includes beer, wine, and well drinks. Premium spirits and craft cocktails will push you toward the higher end. Drink tickets run $8-15 each depending on what's included, while cash bars cost the company nothing (though may cost you in employee goodwill).

### Q: How much alcohol should I order for a corporate event?

A: A good rule of thumb is 2 drinks per person for the first hour, then 1 drink per person per hour after that. For a 100-person, 3-hour event, that's about 400-500 drinks total. We help calculate exactly what you need based on your guest list and event duration.

### Q: Can I mix open bar and cash bar options?

A: Absolutely! Many Austin companies do a hybrid model—open bar for beer and wine, cash bar for premium spirits. Or open bar for the first 2 hours, then cash bar. This gives you budget control while still being generous.

### Q: Do I need a licensed bartender for my corporate event in Austin?

A: Yes, Texas law requires licensed bartenders for events serving alcohol, even private corporate events. We provide TABC-certified bartenders with all our service packages to keep you compliant and your guests safe.

### Q: What's the lead time for booking bar service in Austin?

A: For most corporate events, we recommend booking 3-4 weeks in advance. Holiday parties (November-December) and SXSW season (March) should be booked 6-8 weeks out as venues and services book up fast.

## Making Your Decision: A Simple Framework

Still not sure which option is right for your event? Ask yourself these three questions:

1. **What's the event's purpose?**
   - Client-facing or appreciation event? → Open Bar
   - Team building or casual mixer? → Drink Tickets
   - Large all-staff announcement? → Cash Bar or Hybrid

2. **What's your budget per person?**
   - $40+? → Open Bar with premium options
   - $20-40? → Drink Tickets or basic open bar
   - Under $20? → Cash Bar with company-funded first round

3. **How long is your event?**
   - 2 hours or less? → Open Bar works great
   - 3-4 hours? → Drink Tickets are ideal
   - 4+ hours? → Hybrid model prevents budget bloat
