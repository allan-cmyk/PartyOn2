# How to Plan a Brewery Crawl on Austin's East Side

East Austin has become the city's craft beer mecca, with some of the most innovative breweries in Texas calling this eclectic neighborhood home. Whether you're planning a bachelor party or just looking for a fun Saturday with friends, an East Austin brewery crawl is the perfect way to experience Austin's thriving craft beer scene. Here's your complete guide to planning an epic East Side beer adventure.

## Getting Started: Planning Your Route

The key to a successful brewery crawl is strategic planning. East Austin's breweries are relatively close together, but you'll want to map out your route to minimize walking time (especially during those toasty Texas summers). Here's our recommended crawl order that hits the best spots while keeping things manageable:

* Start point: [Lazarus Brewing Co.](https://www.lazarusbrewing.com/) (1902 E 6th St)
* Second stop: [Zilker Brewing Company](https://zilkerbeer.com/) (1701 E 6th St)
* Third stop: [Central Machine Works](https://www.cmwbrewery.com/) (4824 E Cesar Chavez St)
* Fourth stop: [Meanwhile Brewing Co.](https://meanwhilebeer.com/) (3901 Promontory Point Dr)
* Final destination: [Hops & Grain Brewing](https://www.hopsandgrain.com/) (507 Calles St)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How much should I budget per person for a brewery crawl?

A: Plan for $25-35 per person for beer tastings at each brewery (3-4 samples per stop). Add $20-30 for food and $30-40 for transportation. Total budget: $75-105 per person for a full crawl experience.

### Q: What's the best day for an East Austin brewery crawl?

A: Saturdays offer the best atmosphere with all breweries open and food trucks present. However, Sundays are less crowded and some breweries offer special Sunday deals.

### Q: How many breweries should we visit in one crawl?

A: 4-5 breweries is ideal for a 6-8 hour crawl. This allows enough time to truly enjoy each location without rushing or overconsumption.
