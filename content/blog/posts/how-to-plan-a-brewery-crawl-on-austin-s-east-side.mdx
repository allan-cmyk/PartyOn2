# How to Plan a Brewery Crawl on Austin's East Side

Looking to explore Austin's thriving craft beer scene? East Austin has become the city's unofficial brewery district, with over a dozen top-notch breweries packed into just a few square miles. Whether you're planning a bachelor party, team outing, or just a fun weekend with friends, we'll show you how to plan the perfect East Austin brewery crawl that hits all the best spots while keeping everyone safe and hydrated.

## Why East Austin for Your Brewery Crawl?

East Austin's industrial-meets-artistic vibe has made it the perfect home for craft breweries. With converted warehouses, spacious outdoor patios, and a laid-back atmosphere, the east side offers an authentic Austin beer experience that's hard to beat. Plus, the relatively close proximity of breweries makes it ideal for walking or short rides between venues.

## Planning Your Route

The key to a successful brewery crawl is strategic routing. Here's our recommended itinerary:

1. Start at Lazarus Brewing (they serve coffee and breakfast tacos!)
2. Walk to Zilker Brewing (0.2 miles)
3. Grab a rideshare to Southern Heights (2.5 miles)
4. End at Hops & Grain (1.5 miles)

Pro tip: Party On Delivery can arrange dedicated transportation between breweries, including our popular "Brew Bus" service for groups of 8+.

## Timing and Group Management

![Content Image](/images/blog/how-to-plan-a-brewery-crawl-on-austin-s-east-side/how-to-plan-a-brewery-crawl-on-austin-s-east-side-1.webp)


For the optimal experience:

• Allow 45-60 minutes per brewery
• Start no earlier than 12pm (most breweries open at noon)
• Plan for 3-4 hours total
• Keep groups under 15 people for easier seating
• Make reservations for groups of 8+ (especially weekends)

## Food Strategy

Don't forget to fuel up! Here's our tested food game plan:

• Start with breakfast tacos at Lazarus
• Hit food trucks at Zilker (rotating schedule)
• Order delivery to Southern Heights
• End with snacks at Hops & Grain

## Tips for a Successful Crawl

![Content Image](/images/blog/how-to-plan-a-brewery-crawl-on-austin-s-east-side/how-to-plan-a-brewery-crawl-on-austin-s-east-side-2.webp)


• Assign a designated organizer
• Share contact info for all participants
• Bring water bottles (most breweries offer free fills)
• Pace yourself - sample sizes are your friend
• Get stamps/tokens from each brewery (many offer rewards)
• Take photos at each stop for memories
• Consider weather (many spaces are outdoor-only)

## Frequently Asked Questions

![Content Image](/images/blog/how-to-plan-a-brewery-crawl-on-austin-s-east-side/how-to-plan-a-brewery-crawl-on-austin-s-east-side-3.webp)


### Q: How many breweries should we visit in one crawl?
A: For most groups, 3-4 breweries is ideal. This allows enough time to enjoy each location without rushing, while keeping the total time to 3-4 hours. Any more can be overwhelming and less enjoyable.

### Q: Do we need reservations at East Austin breweries?
A: For groups of 8 or more, reservations are strongly recommended, especially on weekends. Some breweries require them for large groups. Smaller groups can usually find space, but weekend peak hours may have wait times.

### Q: What's the best day for a brewery crawl?
A: Saturday afternoons are most popular but also most crowded. Sunday afternoons tend to be more relaxed. For the best experience with minimal crowds, try Friday afternoon or early Saturday.
