## Finding the Best Tailgate Zones

Once you've nabbed your parking spot, it's time to scope out the ideal tailgating zone. The university has designated several prime areas around the stadium for pre-game revelry, each with its own unique vibe and amenities.

### Longhorn City Limits
Located on the east side of the stadium, [Longhorn City Limits](https://longhornliveevents.com/) is the ultimate tailgating hub for UT fans. This vibrant festival-style atmosphere features food trucks, live music, and plenty of room to set up your tailgate. Just be sure to arrive early, as this popular zone fills up fast.

### Bevo Blvd
Transforming San Jacinto Blvd into a gameday pedestrian plaza, [Bevo Blvd](https://longhornliveevents.com/bevo-blvd/) offers a more intimate tailgating experience. Grab a spot along the tree-lined street, fire up the grill, and enjoy a cold one from Party On Delivery as you watch the Longhorn band march by.

### Manor Lot
For a quieter, more family-friendly tailgate, head to the [Manor Lot](https://parking.utexas.edu/events/football) on the north side of the stadium. This spacious area is perfect for setting up cornhole, lawn games, and a casual cookout with your crew.

## Navigating UT Campus on Gameday

With thousands of fans flocking to campus, getting around on gameday can be a real challenge. To avoid the chaos, familiarize yourself with the campus layout and plan your route in advance.

Here are a few insider tips for navigating UT Austin on football Saturdays:

• Use public transportation when possible. [CapMetro](https://capmetro.org/) offers special gameday routes and park & ride options to shuttle fans directly to the stadium.

• If driving, map out your route beforehand and be prepared for heavy traffic, especially around the I-35 corridor and Martin Luther King Jr. Blvd.

• Consider dropping off passengers and gear at your tailgate zone before finding parking. The [Longhorn Charter Bus](https://www.longhorn-charter.com/) offers convenient shuttles to and from the lots.

• Familiarize yourself with the campus footpaths, crosswalks, and entry/exit points to avoid the madding crowds. The university has a handy [interactive map](https://www.utexas.edu/maps/) to help plan your gameday route.

• Wear comfortable shoes and be prepared to do a lot of walking. Tailgating is just the start - you'll want to leave time to explore the campus festivities before kickoff.
