## What to Wear and Pack for an Austin Boat Party

Ahh, the joys of a good old-fashioned Austin boat party! Whether you're setting sail on Lake Travis, cruising down the Colorado River, or partying it up on Lake Austin, one thing's for sure - you're in for an unforgettable time. But before you can dive into the festivities, you've gotta make sure you've got the right outfit and essentials packed. 

As a proud Austinite, I'm here to give you the inside scoop on how to dress and what to bring to make the most of your boozy boat adventure. So grab a cold one, put on your best Texas-inspired attire, and let's get this party started!

## What to Wear for an Austin Boat Party

![Hero Image](/images/hero/lake-travis-yacht-sunset.webp)


First things first, let's talk about the perfect boat party fit. In a city like Austin, where the vibe is laid-back and the weather is (mostly) cooperatin', you'll want to keep it casual and comfortable. 

Think lightweight, breathable fabrics that can stand up to a little splash action. Opt for swim trunks or lightweight shorts paired with a casual button-down or graphic tee. And don't forget the all-important boat shoes - something with good traction that can handle getting a little wet.

Of course, no Austin boat party look is complete without the right accessories. Slap on a stylish pair of polarized sunglasses to shield your eyes from the glare off the water. And a classic baseball cap or wide-brimmed sun hat will help keep the rays at bay.

Oh, and ladies - a cute sundress or romper is always a safe bet. Just make sure it's something you can easily move around in. You'll be dancing, lounging, and maybe even jumping in the water, so comfort is key.

## What to Pack for an Austin Boat Party

Alright, now that you've got the perfect outfit picked out, let's talk about the essential items to pack for your big boat bash. Here's a handy checklist to make sure you don't forget a thing:

- Swimsuit (and a spare if you plan on getting really wet)
- Towel or two
- Sunscreen (the Texas sun is no joke!)
- Water bottle or reusable tumbler
- Portable phone charger
- Small cooler with your favorite beverages
- Snacks (think easy-to-share finger foods)
- Portable speaker for your party playlist
- Disposable cameras (for those classic boat party polaroids)
- Cash for tips or on-shore excursions
- Small bag or fanny pack to keep essentials close

## Boat Party Etiquette and Safety Tips

Before you set sail, there are a few important etiquette and safety tips to keep in mind:

- Always wear a life jacket when the boat is in motion
- Designate a sober driver or hire a captain to navigate
- Avoid excessive drinking and be mindful of your alcohol intake
- Clean up after yourself and leave no trace behind
- Respect the rules and regulations of the marina or dock you're using
- Be courteous to other boaters and water-goers around you

Following these simple guidelines will help ensure you and your crew have a smooth, safe, and responsible boat party experience.
