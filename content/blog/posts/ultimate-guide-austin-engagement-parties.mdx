## Engagement Party Etiquette

### Traditional Guidelines

While modern engagement parties are flexible, some traditions remain:

- **Who hosts**: Traditionally, the bride's parents host, but today anyone can host including the couple themselves
- **Guest list**: Only invite people who will be invited to the wedding
- **Timing**: Usually within 3 months of the engagement
- **Gifts**: Not expected but often given; registry can be shared if asked

### Modern Approaches

Today's engagement parties are more relaxed:

- Co-hosting between families is common
- Multiple parties in different cities for far-flung guests
- Casual gatherings are just as appropriate as formal events
- The couple can absolutely host their own celebration

## Food and Beverage Planning

### Menu Styles

Choose what fits your celebration:

**Passed Appetizers**: Elegant, mingling-friendly
- Perfect for cocktail-style parties
- Plan 8-12 pieces per person for 2-hour party

**Buffet Style**: Crowd-pleasing variety
- More casual atmosphere
- Easy for guests to serve themselves

**Seated Dinner**: Formal and intimate
- Best for smaller guest counts
- Allows for toasts during meal

**Food Stations**: Interactive and fun
- Different cuisines at each station
- Guests can customize their plates

### Beverage Options

For your adult guests, consider:

- **Champagne toast**: Essential for the celebration
- **Signature cocktails**: One "his" and one "hers" drink
- **Wine selection**: Red and white options
- **Beer choices**: Local Austin craft options
- **Non-alcoholic**: Mocktails and sparkling water

## The Toast

### Who Gives Toasts

Traditional order of toasts:

1. **Host**: Welcomes guests, introduces couple
2. **Parents**: Either or both sets of parents
3. **Best friend/Maid of honor/Best man preview**: Close friends
4. **The couple**: Thank guests and share excitement

### Toast Tips

- Keep it brief (2-3 minutes maximum)
- Share a short story or memory
- End with a wish for the couple's future
- Practice beforehand
- Have champagne ready for all guests

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How soon after getting engaged should we have an engagement party?

A: Most engagement parties happen within 1-3 months of the engagement. This gives you time to plan while the excitement is still fresh. However, there's no strict rule, especially if you need to coordinate with out-of-town guests.

### Q: Who should be invited to an engagement party?

A: Only invite people who will also be invited to the wedding. This includes close family, wedding party members, and good friends. It's awkward to invite someone to the engagement party but not the wedding.

### Q: Is it appropriate to have multiple engagement parties?

A: Yes! It's common to have multiple celebrations, especially if families live in different cities or if you want both a formal family event and a casual friends gathering.

### Q: Should we have a registry for the engagement party?

A: Traditionally, gifts aren't expected at engagement parties, so you don't need to register specifically for this event. However, if guests ask, it's fine to share your wedding registry.

### Q: Can the couple host their own engagement party?

A: Absolutely! While traditionally someone else hosts, it's completely acceptable for the couple to host their own celebration. Many couples prefer this for the flexibility it provides.
