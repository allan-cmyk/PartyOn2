## The Rites of Passage: UT Campus Tailgating

![Hero Image](/images/hero/austin-skyline-hero.webp)


No UT football experience is complete without taking part in the longstanding campus tailgating traditions. The most iconic spot is undoubtedly Bevo Blvd, the official tailgating zone located just outside Darrell K Royal-Texas Memorial Stadium. Here, you'll find hordes of Longhorns fans clad in burnt orange, firing up the grill, and sipping on local brews as they eagerly await the big game.

Just a stone's throw away, another must-visit tailgating hub is the LBJ Lawn, named after former US President and UT alum Lyndon B. Johnson. This expansive green space transforms into a sea of jubilant revelry, with pop-up tents, cornhole tournaments, and the occasional appearance of the beloved Bevo mascot.

For a more exclusive experience, consider booking a spot at one of the university's official tailgating lots, like the Longhorn Alumni Association lot or the UT Parking and Transportation Services lots. These offer premium parking, access to private restrooms, and the opportunity to rub elbows with fellow diehard fans.

## Downtown Delights: Tailgating in the Heart of Austin

While the campus tailgating scene is a rite of passage, Austin also boasts a vibrant downtown area with plenty of tailgating-friendly spots. One of the most popular is [Scholz Garten](https://www.scholzgarten.com/), a historic German beer hall and live music venue that's been a local institution since 1866. On game days, their expansive outdoor space comes alive with fans, food trucks, and a lively atmosphere that's hard to beat.

Just a few blocks away, [Banger's Sausage House & Beer Garden](https://bangersaustin.com/) offers another prime tailgating destination. This spacious beer garden is renowned for its extensive craft beer selection and delectable sausages, making it the perfect spot to fuel up before the big game.

For a more elevated tailgating experience, consider booking a private event space like [Brazos Hall](https://brazoshall.com/). This versatile venue can accommodate large groups and provides a comfortable, climate-controlled environment complete with catering options and premium beverage packages from Party On Delivery.

## Lakeside Lounging: Tailgating on the Water

If you're looking to combine your Longhorns fandom with some lakeside relaxation, head to the [Oasis on Lake Travis](https://www.oasis-austin.com/). This iconic hilltop restaurant and event space offers panoramic views of the sparkling waters of Lake Travis, making it an idyllic spot to gather with friends and fellow fans.

Arrive early to secure a prime parking spot and then set up your tailgating spread on the Oasis's expansive lawn or rooftop terrace. Sip on refreshing cocktails, indulge in local cuisine, and take in the stunning views of the Texas Hill Country as you gear up for the big game.

For a more intimate lakeside experience, consider renting a boat or pontoon from a local marina like [Emerald Point Marina](https://www.emeraldpointmarina.com/). You can create your own floating tailgating party, complete with a premium beverage package from Party On Delivery, and enjoy the game day festivities from the comfort of the water.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What time should I arrive to secure a good tailgating spot?
A: It's recommended to arrive at least 2-3 hours before kickoff to ensure you can find a prime spot for your tailgating setup. The most popular areas, like Bevo Blvd and the LBJ Lawn, tend to fill up quickly on game days.

### Q: Can I bring my own alcohol to the tailgating areas?
A: Yes, you are generally allowed to bring your own alcoholic beverages to the campus tailgating zones and downtown venues. However, some locations may have specific policies or restrictions, so it's always a good idea to check ahead of time.

### Q: Do the tailgating areas have restrooms available?
A: Most of the official UT tailgating lots and downtown venues have portable restrooms or access to indoor facilities. However, it's a good idea to plan ahead and scope out the restroom situation before setting up your tailgating spot.

### Q: What's the best way to get to the tailgating areas?
A: Traffic can be quite heavy on game days, so it's recommended to explore alternative transportation options. Consider taking the [CapMetro](https://www.capmetro.org/) bus or [MetroRail](https://www.capmetro.org/metrorail) train, or coordinate with a rideshare service like Uber or Lyft to avoid the hassle of parking.
