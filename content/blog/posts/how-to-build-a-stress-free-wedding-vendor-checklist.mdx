## Determine Your Wedding Vision

![Hero Image](/images/hero/wedding-hero-garden.webp)


Before you start reaching out to potential vendors, it's important to have a clear vision for your big day. Ask yourself questions like: What's the overall vibe I want to create? Formal or casual? Outdoorsy or urban chic? What's my budget? Knowing these details upfront will help you find the right vendors to bring your dream wedding to life.

## Research and Book Your Venue 

The venue is the foundation of your wedding, so securing the right space should be one of your top priorities. Here in Austin, we're spoiled for choice when it comes to stunning event venues. Some of my personal favorites include:

- [Fairmont Austin](https://www.fairmont.com/austin/): Elegant downtown hotel with breathtaking skyline views
- [Brazos Hall](https://brazoshall.com/): Historic music hall-turned-event space with a cool, industrial vibe
- [The Oasis on Lake Travis](https://www.oasis-austin.com/): Lakeside oasis with unbeatable sunset panoramas

Once you've found your dream venue, be sure to ask about their preferred vendor lists and any in-house services they offer, like catering or bar service. ## Assemble Your Dream Team

With your venue locked in, it's time to start building out your all-star vendor lineup. Here are the key players you'll need to round up:

- Photographer
- Videographer 
- Caterer
- Florist
- Baker (for the cake!)
- DJ or band
- Day-of coordinator

The key is to start researching and booking these vendors as early as possible. Popular Austin wedding pros get booked up fast, especially during peak season. Get a head start by checking out online reviews, portfolios, and pricing.

## Finalize the Details

As you're locking in your vendors, be sure to discuss all the nitty-gritty details:
- Ceremony/reception timelines
- Specific service offerings 
- Vendor load-in/setup requirements
- Rentals needed (tables, linens, etc.)
- Payment schedules and due dates

Creating a detailed checklist for each vendor will ensure nothing falls through the cracks. Don't forget to ask about backup plans in case of emergencies, too.

## Communicate, Communicate, Communicate

Open and frequent communication is the secret sauce to a stress-free wedding planning process. Schedule regular check-ins with your vendors to keep everyone aligned and on the same page. Provide them with your wedding day timeline and any special requests or needs. 

It's also a good idea to designate a point person (like your day-of coordinator) to handle all vendor communications on the big day. This will allow you to be present and fully enjoy your wedding without having to constantly field questions or put out fires.

## Build in Buffers

As you're putting together your wedding day schedule, make sure to build in plenty of breathing room. Unexpected delays are inevitable, so padding your timeline with extra time between events will prevent a domino effect of chaos. 

For example, leave at least 30 minutes between your ceremony and reception to allow for photos, guest mingling, and vendor transitions. And be sure to give your transportation provider ample time to shuttle everyone between venues.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How far in advance should I book my wedding vendors?
A: It's best to start booking your key vendors 9-12 months out, especially for in-demand professionals and popular venues. This will ensure you secure your first-choice vendors and have ample time to iron out all the details. More last-minute bookings (6 months or less) may be possible, but your options may be more limited.

### Q: What's the best way to keep track of all my wedding vendor contracts?
A: Create a dedicated wedding binder or digital folder to store all your vendor agreements, deposits, and important contact info. Having everything in one organized place will make it easy to reference and keep tabs on deadlines and payments.

### Q: Do I need to hire a day-of coordinator?
A: While not strictly required, we highly recommend hiring a professional day-of coordinator to manage your vendor team and ensure your wedding day runs smoothly. Their expertise and organizational skills can be invaluable in keeping you relaxed and present on your big day.

### Q: How do I handle vendor tips?
A: Most vendors expect a tip, typically 15-20% of the total service fee. Be sure to budget for this when finalizing your contracts. You can choose to tip each vendor individually or have your coordinator handle tip envelopes on the day of.
