## Photography Tips by Location

### Golden Hour Planning

Maximize natural light:

- **West-facing locations**: Perfect for sunset shots
- **East-facing**: Beautiful morning sessions
- **Shaded areas**: Good for midday if needed
- **Water reflection**: Doubles the light quality

### Seasonal Considerations

**Spring (March-May)**:
- Wildflower backdrops (bluebonnets)
- Green hills, mild temperatures
- Occasional rain showers

**Summer (June-August)**:
- Harsh midday light, shoot early or late
- Lake activities as backdrop
- Heat considerations for attire

**Fall (September-November)**:
- Golden grasses and changing leaves
- Comfortable temperatures
- Best overall conditions

**Winter (December-February)**:
- Soft light, fewer crowds
- Green cedars, bare oaks
- Unpredictable weather

## Working with Your Photographer

### Pre-Wedding Planning

Discuss location strategy:

- First look location options
- Bridal party photo spots
- Family formal locations
- Sunset timing for couple portraits

### Day-Of Timeline

Build in travel and photo time:

- Allow 30-60 minutes for location changes
- Schedule portraits during best light
- Plan backup indoor options
- Consider guests waiting at reception

## Permits and Logistics

### Public Parks

Many Austin parks require photography permits:

- Zilker Park and gardens
- Lady Bird Wildflower Center
- Mount Bonnell (limited commercial use)
- Hamilton Pool (reservation required)

### Private Property

Coordinate with venues:

- Discuss available photo locations
- Ask about additional access areas
- Confirm timeline with venue coordinator
- Consider off-site options nearby

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How early should we book popular outdoor locations?

A: For venues like Hamilton Pool or popular wedding venues, book 9-12 months ahead. Public parks with permit requirements need 2-4 weeks notice.

### Q: What if it rains on our wedding day?

A: Austin weather can be unpredictable. Have indoor backup locations identified, and trust your photographer to find beautiful light in any condition.

### Q: Can we visit multiple locations for photos?

A: Yes, but plan carefully. Moving between locations takes time and can add stress. Two locations maximum works well for most timelines.
