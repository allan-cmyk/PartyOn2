## Eco-Friendly Tailgating: How to Clean Up Responsibly

Austin is known for its vibrant tailgating scene, especially when it comes to UT football games. From the iconic Longhorn logo to the sea of burnt orange, Austinites know how to throw an unforgettable tailgate party. But with all the revelry and excitement, it's important to remember our responsibility to the environment. 

As an Austin-based premium alcohol delivery service specializing in events like weddings, bachelor/bachelorette parties, and corporate shindigs, we at [Party On Delivery](https://partyondelivery.com/) have seen our fair share of tailgates. That's why we're here to share our top tips for hosting an eco-friendly tailgate that's fun, sustainable, and respectful of our beloved city.

## Ditch the Disposables

![Hero Image](/images/hero/austin-skyline-hero.webp)


It may be tempting to stock up on paper plates, plastic cups, and single-use everything for your tailgate, but those items quickly add up to a mountain of waste. Instead, opt for reusable dinnerware, glassware, and utensils. [Daytrip](https://www.daytriprentals.com/) in downtown Austin offers a great selection of rental items perfect for tailgating. 

Another handy tip? Invest in a few sturdy, reusable beverage containers. [Yeti](https://www.yeti.com/) coolers and tumblers are incredibly durable and keep your drinks cold all day long. Not only are they better for the environment, but they'll also elevate the overall look and feel of your tailgate setup.

## Master the Art of Recycling

No eco-friendly tailgate is complete without a stellar recycling system. Before the big game, make sure to stock up on clearly labeled recycling bins and strategically place them around your tailgate area. Encourage your guests to properly sort their waste, and consider designating a "recycling captain" to oversee the process.

For hard-to-recycle items like glass bottles and aluminum cans, plan ahead by finding a nearby recycling center that accepts tailgating materials. The [Balcones Recycling](https://www.balconesrecycling.com/) facility just off I-35 in North Austin is a great option.

## Reduce Food Waste

Ah, the age-old tailgating conundrum: how much food is too much? To avoid an excess of uneaten snacks and leftovers, try to plan your menu carefully. Make a list of all the items you'll need, and be realistic about portion sizes. 

If you do end up with leftovers, get creative! Turn that extra chili into a hearty soup for your next game day gathering, or repurpose any unused buns or bread for homemade croutons or breadcrumbs. And don't forget to compost any fruit and veggie scraps - the [Austin Resource Recovery](https://austintexas.gov/department/austin-resource-recovery) program makes it easy to get started.

## Clean Up Thoroughly

When the final seconds tick off the clock and it's time to head home, take the time to thoroughly clean up your tailgate area. Gather any remaining trash and recyclables, and make sure to leave no trace of your celebration. 

For large items like tents, tables, and coolers, consider renting a [Longhorn Charter Bus](https://www.longhorn-charter.com/) to make the load-out process a breeze. And if you need to dispose of any hazardous materials like used cooking oil or batteries, the [Austin Hazardous Waste Facility](https://www.austintexas.gov/department/hazardous-waste-facility) is the perfect resource.

## Spread the Word

As passionate Austinites, we know how important it is to protect our beautiful city. So once you've perfected your eco-friendly tailgating routine, be sure to share your tips and tricks with fellow UT fans. Encourage your tailgating crew to adopt sustainable practices, and lead by example whenever you hit the parking lot.

Together, we can preserve the magic of Texas Longhorns football while ensuring a greener, cleaner future for all.
