# Best Tailgate Food and BBQ Recipes for Longhorn Fans

As an Austin-based Longhorns superfan, I know a thing or two about throwing the ultimate tailgate party. Whether you're gearing up for a big game at [Darrell K Royal-Texas Memorial Stadium](https://texassports.com/sports/2018/6/15/facilities-darrell-k-royal-texas-memorial-stadium-html.aspx) or hosting friends for a backyard viewing bash, the food and drinks are just as important as the action on the field.

In this guide, I'm sharing my go-to tailgate recipes, local Austin restaurant recommendations, and insider tips to make your Longhorns game day celebration a touchdown. Let's dive in!

## Texas-Style Barbecue Essentials

No tailgate is complete without classic Texas BBQ. And in Austin, we take our 'cue seriously. Start with the basics - succulent brisket, fall-off-the-bone ribs, and juicy sausage links. My personal favorite spot for game day takeout is [Franklin Barbecue](https://franklinbbq.com/), where the lines are always long but the 'que is worth the wait.

If you're feeling ambitious, you can also make your own Texas-style barbecue at home. Rub down those briskets and ribs with a savory blend of spices, then slow smoke them over fragrant post oak wood. Pair with homemade sides like creamy mac and cheese, tangy coleslaw, and buttery cornbread.

## Tailgate-Friendly Appetizers

![Hero Image](/images/hero/austin-skyline-hero.webp)


Of course, you can't have a Longhorns tailgate without some classic game day appetizers. Kick things off with a towering plate of loaded nachos, piled high with melty cheddar, spicy chili, fresh salsa, and all the fixings. For an extra-special twist, try swapping in brisket or pulled pork for the chili.

Another fan favorite? Crispy chicken wings tossed in a sticky-sweet barbecue sauce or fiery buffalo glaze. Serve them up with cool, creamy ranch or tangy blue cheese dressing for dipping. Pro tip: Fry the wings ahead of time, then just reheat and sauce them at the tailgate for maximum crispiness.

## Festive Longhorns Cocktails

Of course, no tailgate is complete without a signature cocktail or two! Pay homage to the Longhorns with a boozy burnt orange punch, complete with vodka, orange juice, and a hint of cinnamon. Or get the party started with a round of ice-cold Shiner Bocks - the official beer of Texas.

For a fun and easy DIY bar, set up a Longhorns-themed margarita station with all the fixings. Rim glasses with a mix of salt and chili powder, then let guests mix their own margs with freshly squeezed lime juice, orange liqueur, and of course, plenty of tequila. Don't forget the garnishes - a skewer of chili-lime fruit makes for a festive finishing touch.

## Longhorns Tailgating Essentials

Beyond the food and drinks, there are a few other must-haves for your Longhorns tailgate. Make sure to pack a portable speaker for pumping up the tunes, and don't forget the cornhole set for some friendly competition. And of course, stock up on all your burnt orange gear - from jerseys and hats to flags and koozies.

If you really want to take your tailgate game to the next level, consider renting a party bus or limo from a local company like [Longhorn Charter Bus](https://www.longhorn-charter.com/). That way, you and your crew can arrive in style and leave the driving to someone else. Just make sure to book early, as these options tend to fill up fast on game days.
