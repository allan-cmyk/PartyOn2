## Tailgate Playlist Ideas to Hype Your Crowd

As an Austin-based premium alcohol delivery service, we know a thing or two about throwing the ultimate tailgate party. Whether you're gearing up for a [UT Longhorns](https://utexas.edu/) game at [Darrell K Royal-Texas Memorial Stadium](https://dkrws.com/) or hosting a lively corporate event, the right music is key to setting the perfect vibe and hyping up your crowd.

From classic Texas country to pumped-up anthems, we've curated a selection of tailgate playlist ideas that are sure to get your guests dancing, singing, and ready to party. So crank up the volume, grab an ice-cold [Lone Star](https://www.lonestarbrewing.com/) or [Shiner Bock](https://www.shiner.com/), and get ready to show off your tailgating skills.

## Country Classics

![Hero Image](/images/blog/tailgate-playlist-ideas-to-hype-your-crowd/tailgate-playlist-ideas-to-hype-your-crowd-0.webp)

No Texas tailgate is complete without a healthy dose of country music. Lean into the laid-back, feel-good vibe with a mix of timeless hits and new favorites:

- "Chattahoochee" by Alan Jackson
- "Amarillo by Morning" by George Strait
- "Watermelon Crawl" by Tracy Byrd
- "Drunk on You" by Luke Bryan
- "Dirt Road Anthem" by Jason Aldean

## Pump-Up Anthems
When the energy starts to lag, rev up the crowd with some high-energy, fist-pumping anthems that will have everyone singing (or shouting) along:

- "Don't Stop Believin'" by Journey
- "Sweet Caroline" by Neil Diamond
- "Sweet Home Alabama" by Lynyrd Skynyrd
- "Party in the U.S.A." by Miley Cyrus
- "Thunderstruck" by AC/DC

## Tailgating Timeline Tips
When it comes to planning the perfect tailgate, timing is everything. Here are a few insider tips to keep in mind:

- Arrive early (at least 3-4 hours before kickoff) to claim your spot and set up
- Book your venue or event space well in advance, especially for big games
- Order any catering, alcohol, or delivery services at least 1-2 weeks ahead
- Don't forget to factor in transportation and parking considerations

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What should I include in a tailgate playlist?
A: A great tailgate playlist should have a mix of upbeat, crowd-pleasing hits, classic rock anthems, and some local/regional favorites to get the crowd hyped up. Aim for a balance of familiar sing-alongs and pump-up songs to keep the energy high.

### Q: How long should a tailgate playlist be?
A: For a typical 3-4 hour tailgate, you'll want a playlist that's at least 2-3 hours long. This will give you enough variety to keep the music flowing without too much repetition. You can also build in some flexibility by creating multiple playlists or having a DJ on hand to mix it up.

### Q: What are some tips for planning a successful tailgate party?
A: In addition to a killer playlist, some other key tips for a successful tailgate include:
- Arrive early to claim your spot and set up
- Bring all the essentials (coolers, portable speakers, lawn games, etc.)
- Order catering, alcohol delivery, and any other services in advance
- Designate a group leader to coordinate logistics and keep things running smoothly
- Encourage attendees to dress up in team colors and get into the spirit
