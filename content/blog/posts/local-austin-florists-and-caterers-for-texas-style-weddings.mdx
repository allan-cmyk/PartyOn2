## Stunning Florals from Austin's Top Florists

![Hero Image](/images/hero/wedding-hero-garden.webp)


No Texas wedding is complete without jaw-dropping floral displays. Luckily, Austin is home to a thriving community of skilled florists who can transform any space into a botanical oasis.

[Stems Floral Design](https://stemsfloral.com/) is a local favorite known for their elevated, one-of-a-kind arrangements. Owner Shea Jameson and her team have an impeccable eye for color and texture, creating everything from elegant ceremony backdrops to vibrant reception centerpieces.

For a more rustic, Hill Country-inspired look, check out [Bouquets of Austin](https://www.boquetsofaustin.com/). Owner Gabi Valdez sources locally-grown blooms whenever possible, showcasing the natural beauty of Texas flora. They excel at lush, organic-style bouquets and ceremony arches perfect for an outdoor wedding.

Another Austin florist to have on your radar is [Freesia Designs](https://www.freesiadesigns.com/). Their team specializes in bold, statement-making arrangements, including dramatic hanging installations and unique tabletop displays. They're masters at creating custom looks to match any wedding vision.

## Savory Bites from Austin's Top Caterers

Beyond the flowers, food is arguably the most important element of any wedding reception. Luckily, Austin is brimming with award-winning caterers who can serve up mouthwatering, Texas-inspired menus.

For an authentic taste of the Lone Star State, look no further than [Moonshine Patio Bar & Grill](https://www.moonshinegrill.com/). This beloved local institution is known for its elevated takes on classic Southern fare, from smoked brisket to jalapeño mac and cheese. Their experienced catering team can handle weddings of any size.

If you're craving lighter, more modern cuisine, consider [Verts Mediterranean Grill](https://www.vertskebabers.com/). This fast-casual chain started in Austin and has become a local favorite for their fresh, flavor-packed Mediterranean dishes. Their catering menu offers crowd-pleasing options like Greek salad, falafel, and build-your-own pita platters.

For couples seeking an upscale, chef-driven experience, [Jacoby's Restaurant & Mercantile](https://www.jacobysrestaurant.com/) is an excellent choice. Chef Kory Clemens (son of baseball legend Roger Clemens) and his team are masters of seasonal, farm-to-table cuisine. Their catered menus feature elevated takes on Texas classics, from mesquite-grilled steaks to locally-sourced produce.

## Finding the Perfect Austin Wedding Venues

Once you've secured your dream floral and catering teams, it's time to start scouting Austin's best wedding venues. The city offers an incredible variety, from historic downtown event spaces to sprawling Hill Country ranches.

For couples seeking an urban, loft-style setting, [Brazos Hall](https://brazoshall.com/) is a top pick. This industrial-chic event space features soaring ceilings, exposed brick walls, and panoramic views of the Austin skyline. Its flexible layout can accommodate weddings of up to 1,000 guests.

Those dreaming of a countryside celebration will love [Camp Lucy](https://www.camplucytx.com/) in Dripping Springs. This 250-acre property features multiple ceremony and reception sites, from the rustic-elegant Ian's Chapel to the open-air Whit's Inn. Their team can help you craft the perfect Hill Country wedding experience.

Another gorgeous Hill Country venue is [The Milestone](https://themilestoneevents.com/) in Kyle. Surrounded by rolling pastures and oak trees, it offers a stunning natural backdrop for saying "I do." Couples rave about the venue's charming historic buildings, including the Homestead and the Carriage House.

## Practical Tips for Planning an Austin Wedding

When it comes to planning an unforgettable Austin wedding, there are a few key tips to keep in mind:

• Start your vendor search early - the best florists, caterers, and venues book up quickly, especially during peak wedding season.

• Consider an "off-season" date (like a Friday or Sunday) to save on venue and vendor costs.

• Be flexible with your decor, as sourcing certain flowers or rental items can be challenging in the Texas heat.

• Don't forget the beverages! Work with a local alcohol delivery service like [Party On Delivery](https://partyondelivery.com/weddings) to handle your wedding bar and ensure a seamless reception.

• Allow extra time in your timeline for traffic and Austin's notorious "construction season" if you're hosting downtown.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How far in advance should I book my Austin wedding vendors?
A: We recommend starting your vendor search at least 12-18 months out, especially for popular venues, florists, and caterers. The most sought-after options can book up 1-2 years in advance during peak wedding season. Be flexible with your date if possible, and lock in your top choices as early as you can.

### Q: What are the most popular wedding styles and themes in Austin?
A: Austin weddings run the gamut from rustic-chic Hill Country affairs to sleek, modern downtown celebrations. Some of the most popular themes include Hill Country-inspired, Texas-meets-boho, industrial-loft, and classic Southern elegance. No matter your style, you can find vendors who can bring your vision to life.

### Q: How much should I budget for an Austin wedding?
A: The average cost of a wedding in the Austin metro area is around $30,000-$35,000. However, budgets can vary widely depending on your guest count, venue, catering, and other factors. As a general guideline, plan to allocate 30-40% of your total budget for the venue, 20-25% for catering and bar service, 10-15% for florals, and 5-10% for photography/videography.
