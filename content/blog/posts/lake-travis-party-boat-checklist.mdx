### Non-Alcoholic Essentials

Critical for hydration:

- Water bottles (4-6 per person)
- Sports drinks (2-3 per person)
- Sodas for mixing
- Juice for cocktails
- Energy drinks (optional)

### Ice and Coolers

- 2 bags of ice minimum per cooler
- Separate cooler for food vs. drinks
- Backup ice (buy extra, you'll use it)
- Drain plugs accessible

Party On Delivery delivers everything above directly to your door.

## Food Checklist

### Easy Boat Foods

No utensils required:

- Sandwiches (pre-wrapped)
- Tortilla wraps
- Chips and dip containers
- Cut fruit in containers
- Cheese and cracker packs
- Trail mix or nuts
- Granola/protein bars
- Cookies or brownies

### Food Prep Tips

- Pre-cut everything at home
- Use resealable containers
- Pack napkins and paper towels
- Bring trash bags for cleanup
- No glass containers

## Safety Equipment Checklist

### Required Items

Don't leave without these:

- Life jackets (verify boat has enough)
- First aid kit basics
- Waterproof phone case
- Charged phone for emergencies
- Boat rental documentation
- ID for all adults

### Recommended Safety Gear

Extra protection:

- Throwable flotation device
- Whistle or air horn
- Flashlight (for late returns)
- Fire extinguisher (boat should have)

## Sun Protection Checklist

### Sunscreen

Texas sun is intense:

- SPF 30+ sunscreen
- Waterproof formula
- Spray for reapplication
- Lip balm with SPF
- Face sunscreen if preferred

### Coverage

Shade and coverage:

- Wide-brimmed hats
- Sunglasses (with strap!)
- Cover-ups or rash guards
- Beach towels for shade
- Umbrella (some boats have)

## Comfort Items Checklist

### What to Wear

- Swimsuits (bring two for change)
- Dry clothes for after
- Water shoes (deck can be hot)
- Light cover-up
- Baseball cap

### Towels and Comfort

- Beach towels (2 per person)
- Microfiber towels dry fast
- Blanket for evening cooldown
- Pillow for lounging (optional)

## Entertainment Checklist

### Music

Set the vibe:

- Waterproof Bluetooth speaker
- Charged phone/device for music
- Curated playlist downloaded
- Backup charging cable/battery

### Water Toys

Fun on the water:

- Pool floats (bring extras)
- Floating cooler
- Water noodles
- Snorkeling gear
- Underwater camera
- Football or frisbee
- Floating beer pong table

### Games and Activities

- Waterproof playing cards
- Floating dice games
- Bluetooth speaker for music
- Portable phone charger

## Technical Checklist

### Electronics

- Phone in waterproof pouch
- Portable charger
- Waterproof camera
- GoPro and mount
- All chargers

### Important Documents

Bring and protect:

- Driver's license
- Credit card for incidentals
- Boat rental confirmation
- Emergency contact list
- Insurance information

## Weather Preparedness

### Check Before You Go

- Morning weather forecast
- Afternoon storm probability
- Wind conditions
- Water temperature
- UV index

### Weather Gear

Just in case:

- Rain jacket (light)
- Plastic bags for electronics
- Extra towels
- Plan for where to dock if storms

## Morning-Of Checklist

### Before Leaving Home

- Coolers packed and iced
- Sunscreen applied (first coat)
- Phones charged
- Playlist ready
- Weather checked
- Gas tank full

### At the Marina

- Check-in with rental company
- Safety briefing attended
- Verify life jacket count
- Know return time
- Get marina contact number
- Locate emergency equipment

## End of Day Checklist

### Before Docking

- Gather all belongings
- Collect all trash
- Return borrowed items to spots
- Account for all electronics
- Note any damage to report

### At Marina

- Return on time (avoid late fees)
- Remove all personal items
- Dispose of trash properly
- Return keys/equipment
- Get deposit back if applicable

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How much ice do I really need?

A: More than you think. Plan for 2-3 bags per cooler and expect to replenish if possible. Texas heat melts ice fast, especially on the water.

### Q: What's the most commonly forgotten item?

A: Sunscreen reapplication and extra water. People remember to bring them but forget to use enough throughout the day.

### Q: Should I bring a cooler or does the boat have one?

A: Bring your own coolers even if the boat has them. Boat coolers are often smaller than you need for a full day with a group.

### Q: Can Party On Delivery help me prepare?

A: Yes! We deliver all your beverages so you can focus on packing everything else. One less errand to run before your boat day.

## Pro Tips

### The Night Before

- Freeze water bottles (they become ice)
- Pre-make cocktails in pitchers
- Charge all devices overnight
- Pack coolers (except ice)
- Lay out everything to load

### Morning Of

- Load coolers with ice last
- Double-check ID and confirmation
- Take pre-boat photos (you'll look better)
- Eat breakfast (important for day drinking)
- Apply sunscreen before you leave
