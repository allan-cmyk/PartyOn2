## Decide on the Guest List

![Hero Image](/images/blog/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning-0.webp)


The first step in planning an inclusive engagement party is determining your guest list. Start by making a list of your immediate family members and your partner's immediate family. Then, consider close friends and other important people in your lives - think your college roommates, your wedding party, and any special mentors or family friends.

Remember, an engagement party doesn't have to be a huge blowout. In fact, keeping the guest list intimate can make the celebration feel that much more special. Some of our favorite Austin venues for small, personalized engagement parties include [Brazos Hall](https://brazos-hall.com/), [Fairmont Austin](https://www.fairmont.com/austin/), and [The Oasis on Lake Travis](https://www.oasis-austin.com/).

## Choose a Meaningful Location

Speaking of venues, the location you choose for your engagement party can set the whole tone for the event. Are you envisioning something elegant and refined, like a rooftop soiree at the [Fairmont Austin](https://www.fairmont.com/austin/)? Or perhaps a laidback, Texas-style celebration at a rustic spot like [Brazos Hall](https://brazos-hall.com/)?

No matter what vibe you're going for, make sure the venue feels personal and meaningful to you as a couple. Maybe it's the restaurant where you had your first date, or a scenic spot that holds special significance. And of course, don't forget to factor in accessibility for all your guests.

## Get the Whole Family Involved

![Content Image](/images/blog/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning-1.webp)


One of the best ways to make your engagement party feel truly inclusive is to get your family and friends involved in the planning process. Assign specific tasks to your nearest and dearest, whether it's picking out the perfect playlist, sourcing gorgeous florals from a local Austin vendor, or helping to coordinate the timeline for the day.

Not only will this take some of the stress off your plate, but it'll also make your loved ones feel valued and invested in the celebration. Plus, delegating certain responsibilities is a great way to give your family members a sense of ownership over the event.

## Incorporate Meaningful Traditions

Chances are, your family and your partner's family each have their own unique engagement and wedding traditions. Why not incorporate some of those meaningful rituals into your own party? This could be anything from a special cultural dance, to a toast led by your parents, to a family recipe featured on the menu.

Not only will these traditions make your engagement party feel extra personal, but they're also a wonderful way to honor your loved ones and blend your two families together. ## Make It Interactive and Fun

![Content Image](/images/blog/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning-2.webp)


Remember, the whole point of an engagement party is to celebrate your love and bring your favorite people together. So, don't be afraid to get creative and plan some interactive activities that will get your guests mingling and having a blast.

Maybe it's a lively lawn game tournament, a karaoke session, or a DIY photo booth where everyone can snap silly pictures. You could even hire a [Longhorn Charter Bus](https://www.longhorncharterbus.com/) to ferry your guests to a second location for after-party festivities. The key is to strike a balance between structured events and open-ended fun.

## Share the Love on Social Media

In the age of Instagram and TikTok, no engagement celebration is complete without a healthy dose of social media sharing. Encourage your guests to snap photos and videos throughout the party, and create a custom hashtag (like #JaneAndJohnAreEngaged) so everyone can easily find and engage with the content.

You can even designate a special spot for a photo backdrop or "Instagrammable moment" that guests can use to capture their favorite memories. And of course, make sure to post plenty of your own sweet snaps - after all, this is your moment to shine!

## Frequently Asked Questions

![Content Image](/images/blog/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning/how-to-include-family-and-friends-in-your-engagement-planning-3.webp)


### Q: How long should an engagement party last?
A: There's no set rule for how long an engagement party should last, but most range from 2-4 hours. The exact timeline will depend on factors like the venue, the size of your guest list, and the flow of the event. A general guideline is to plan for 1-2 hours of mingling and cocktails, followed by 1 hour for any structured activities, toasts, or special traditions.

### Q: Do I need to send formal invitations?
A: While formal paper invitations aren't strictly necessary for an engagement party, they can be a nice touch - especially if you're hosting a more formal event. You can find gorgeous Austin-inspired invites from local stationers or even create your own digital invites to email or share on social media. The most important thing is clearly communicating the date, time, location, and any other need-to-know details to your guests.

### Q: How much should I budget for an engagement party?
A: The budget for your engagement party will depend on factors like the size of your guest list, the type of venue you choose, and the level of catering and bar service you want. As a general guideline, you can expect to spend anywhere from $50-$150 per person for a full-service event with food and drinks. [Party On Delivery](https://partyondelivery.com/weddings) offers a variety of engagement party packages and à la carte services to fit every budget.

### Q: When should I start planning my engagement party?
A: We recommend starting the planning process for your engagement party 2-3 months in advance. This will give you ample time to secure your venue, send invitations, and coordinate with any vendors or service providers you want to include. Of course, the more complicated or elaborate your event, the earlier you'll want to start the planning process.
