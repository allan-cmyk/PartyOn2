## SCRIPT 3: Handling Common Questions

### "How does it work?"

```
"Super simple. When someone books an event at your venue, you mention that
we can deliver their alcohol directly to you. They go to our site, order
what they need - we even have a calculator that tells them exactly how much
to get - and we deliver it cold, ready to go, on the day of their event.

If they over-order, they can return unopened bottles for a refund. And you
get a commission on every order that comes to your venue. That's it - no
inventory, no hassle, just passive income."
```

### "What's the commission?"

```
"We have three tiers. Listed Partners get 5%, Featured Partners get 7%,
and Premier Partners get 9%. The higher tiers come with more marketing
benefits - like a dedicated landing page on our site and being featured
in our newsletter.

Most venues start at Featured, which is 7%. If you're doing 8 weddings
a month with an average alcohol order of $1,500, that's about $840 a month
in commission. Just for mentioning us."
```

### "We already recommend another vendor"

```
"Totally understand. A lot of venues recommend multiple options to give
their clients choices. We're not asking to be exclusive - just another
option in your toolkit.

What sets us apart is the quantity calculator - we tell them exactly what
to buy - and the returns policy. They can bring back unopened bottles, so
they're never stuck with extra alcohol.

Would it make sense to at least be listed as an option for clients who
ask for recommendations?"
```

### "We don't get involved with alcohol"

```
"I totally get that. A lot of venues prefer to stay hands-off, which is
fine. We'd just be another vendor option on your list.

Here's what some venues do: they add us to their FAQ or vendor page as a
'convenient option for alcohol delivery.' That's it. You're not endorsing
us or taking responsibility - just pointing people to a resource.

Would something like that work?"
```

### "Is there a contract?"

```
"No long-term contracts. Either party can end the partnership with 30 days
notice. We believe in earning your business, not locking you in.

Most venues start at our basic tier just to try it out. If it works, great
- you can upgrade. If not, no hard feelings."
```

### "Let me think about it"

```
"Absolutely, take your time. Can I send you a quick one-pager that breaks
down the partnership tiers? That way you have something to reference.

And when would be a good time to follow up - maybe next week?"
```

### "Not interested"

```
"No problem at all. I appreciate your time.

Quick question before I go - is it a timing thing, or just not a fit for
how you operate? ... [Listen] ... Got it. Well, if anything changes, we're
at partyondelivery.com. Have a great day!"
```

## Best Practices

### Before the Call
- Research the venue (recent events, IG posts)
- Know their category (wedding, entertainment, etc.)
- Have their website open
- Be ready to send follow-up email immediately

### During the Call
- Stand up (better energy)
- Smile (they can hear it)
- Let them talk - listen for pain points
- Take notes on their concerns

### After the Call
- Log it immediately
- Send follow-up email within 1 hour
- Schedule next touchpoint
- Update CRM/spreadsheet

### Timing
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times:** 10am-12pm or 2pm-4pm
- **Avoid:** Monday mornings, Friday afternoons
