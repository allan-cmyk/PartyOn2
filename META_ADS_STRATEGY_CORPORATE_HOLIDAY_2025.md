# Meta Ads Strategy: Corporate Holiday Party Events 2025
## Party On Delivery - Austin, TX

**Campaign Duration:** 3 weeks (December 2-20, 2025)
**Total Budget:** $400/month (~$13/day)
**Primary Goal:** Generate leads (form submissions) from corporate event planners
**Target Audience:** Office managers, HR professionals, executive assistants, event coordinators
**Landing Page:** partyondelivery.com/corporate

## Executive Summary

This campaign targets Austin-area corporate decision makers planning holiday parties for their teams. Unlike the consumer gift campaign, this B2B approach focuses on **lead generation** rather than direct e-commerce purchases, with the goal of capturing high-value corporate accounts.

**Why Corporate > Individual Gifts:**

| Metric | Gift Campaign | Corporate Campaign |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Avg Order Value | $90 | $500-2,000+ |
| Decision Maker | Personal funds | Company budget |
| Price Sensitivity | High | Low |
| Repeat Potential | 1x/year | Multiple events/year |
| Referral Value | Low | High (other companies) |

**Key Strategy:**
- Focus on **lead capture** (form submissions on /corporate page)
- Target **B2B job titles** (office managers, HR, executive assistants)
- Emphasize **professional benefits** (one invoice, scheduled delivery, TABC licensed)
- Highlight **pain points** (no employee store runs, ice included, capacity for any size)
- Drive urgency with **December 20 delivery deadline**

**Expected Results (3 weeks):**
- 15-25 lead form submissions
- 3-4 converted customers (25% close rate)
- $2,400-3,200 revenue (3-4 orders @ $800 avg)
- ROAS: 6-8x ($2,400-3,200 / $400 spend)

## Troubleshooting Guide

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Issue: Low CTR (< 0.5%)**
- **Cause:** Creative not resonating with B2B audience
- **Fix:** Test professional imagery, emphasize business benefits, add trust signals

**Issue: High CPL (> $50)**
- **Cause:** Audience too broad or competitive timing
- **Fix:** Tighten job title targeting, add company size filter, exclude irrelevant interests

**Issue: Leads Not Responding**
- **Cause:** Wrong audience (consumers vs corporate)
- **Fix:** Review lead quality, tighten targeting to job titles only, add income filter

**Issue: Low Form Completion Rate**
- **Cause:** Form too long or confusing
- **Fix:** Reduce required fields, add progress indicator, test shorter form

**Issue: High Frequency (> 7)**
- **Cause:** Audience exhausted
- **Fix:** Expand audience slightly, refresh creative, pause low performers

**Issue: Ad Rejected**
- **Cause:** Alcohol policy violation
- **Fix:** Ensure age targeting 21+, avoid "party" imagery that looks underage, no excessive drinking references

## Pro Tips for B2B Meta Ads

### What Works for Corporate Audiences

**DO:**
- Lead with business benefits (invoice, scheduling, compliance)
- Include phone number in ad copy (B2B buyers want to talk)
- Use professional imagery (not casual party photos)
- Add trust signals (TABC licensed, insured, years in business)
- Emphasize convenience and time savings
- Target job titles specifically
- Follow up quickly (within 2 hours)
- Use LinkedIn-style language (professional, not casual)

**DON'T:**
- Use overly festive/party imagery (looks unprofessional)
- Focus on price (B2B cares about value, not cheapest)
- Ignore leads for more than 24 hours
- Use consumer-style copy ("AMAZING DEAL!")
- Target too broadly (generic "business" interests)
- Forget to exclude competitors
- Run ads on weekends only (B2B decides M-F)

### B2B vs B2C Creative Differences

| Element | B2C (Gift Campaign) | B2B (Corporate Campaign) |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Imagery | Festive, colorful | Professional, clean |
| Copy Tone | Friendly, casual | Professional, direct |
| CTA | "Shop Now" | "Get Quote" |
| Trust | Reviews, ratings | Licenses, insurance |
| Urgency | "Limited time!" | "Book your date" |
| Benefits | Personal enjoyment | Business convenience |

5. **Higher close rate** - B2B leads with specific dates are serious
